Levels of PMF

引入极致产品市场契合度

Introducing Extreme Product-Market Fit

Let's start with the obvious question: What is product-market fit? Here's our specific definition of what founders are aiming for — and why it's a bit different from what you may have seen before:

让我们从显而易见的问题开始：什么是产品市场契合度？以下是我们对创始人目标的明确定义——以及为什么它与你之前可能看到的有所不同：

极致产品市场契合度是一种状态：对满足关键需求的产品有广泛的需求——关键是——可以重复且高效地交付给每个客户。

Extreme product-market fit is a state of widespread demand for a product that satisfies a critical need and — crucially — can be delivered repeatably and efficiently to each customer.

As we've noted, the way most people have talked about product-market fit feels insufficient. More specifically, there are certain nuances that we feel haven't yet been adequately addressed:

正如我们所指出的，大多数人谈论产品市场契合度的方式感觉不够充分。更具体地说，我们认为某些细微差别尚未得到充分解决：

• Product-market fit progresses in levels. It's not a binary state where you have it and you're golden, or you don't and you're not on the path. But it's also not a vague spectrum or fuzzy sliding scale, where you can't be sure where you're at.

• 产品市场契合度是分层次推进的。它不是一个二元状态，要么你有它就很棒，要么没有就不在正确的道路上。但它也不是一个模糊的光谱或模糊的滑动尺度，你无法确定自己在哪里。

• There are a few dimensions of PMF that are in tension with each other, requiring deliberate tradeoffs at each level. There isn't just one element to emphasize above all else. And importantly, there are different times in a company's lifespan where founders need to intentionally prioritize one over another.

• PMF有几个维度相互之间存在张力，需要在每个层次上进行深思熟虑的权衡。不是只有一个元素需要凌驾于其他所有元素之上。重要的是，在公司生命周期的不同时间，创始人需要有意识地优先考虑一个维度而非另一个。

• There isn't nearly enough emphasis on repeatability. As you move your way up through the different levels of product-market fit, what you're really unlocking is repeatability — across demand generation, product development, customer satisfaction and unit economics.

• 对可重复性的重视远远不够。当你逐步提升产品市场契合度的不同层次时，你真正解锁的是可重复性——跨越需求生成、产品开发、客户满意度和单位经济学。

Repeatability is the holy grail on the hunt for product-market fit. Without any patterns, it's nearly impossible to generate early momentum and plot a course forward.

可重复性是寻找产品市场契合度的圣杯。没有任何模式，几乎不可能产生早期动力并规划前进路线。

That's why when we picture product-market fit, we see three distinct building blocks:

这就是为什么当我们想象产品市场契合度时，我们看到三个不同的构建模块：

Four Levels: Nascent, Developing, Strong, Extreme Product-Market Fit

四个层级：初生、发展、强劲、极致产品市场契合度

Three Dimensions: Satisfaction, Demand, Efficiency

三个维度：满意度、需求、效率

Levers: Change your persona, your problem, your promise, or your product (our version of the 4Ps) to make the work of finding, winning, serving and retaining your marginal customer easier.

杠杆：改变你的用户画像、你的问题、你的承诺或你的产品（我们版本的4P），使寻找、赢得、服务和留住你的边际客户的工作更容易。

产品市场契合度的四个层级

The 4 Levels of Product-Market Fit

We've identified four concrete levels of PMF, with what we're calling extreme product-market fit as the ultimate goal — what's required to build a generational company.

我们确定了PMF的四个具体层级，将我们所称的极致产品市场契合度作为最终目标——这是打造一家世代相传的公司所需要的。

Rather than a blanket 'you'll know it when you have it,' we've found that for enterprise companies there's a clear progression that companies go through that is fairly predictable. Many founders have walked this path, and based on what we've seen from the hundreds of early-stage startups we've backed, this journey to extreme product-market fit usually takes anywhere from two to six years.

我们发现，对于企业公司来说，有一个相当可预测的公司经历的明确进展，而不是笼统的当你有了它你就会知道。许多创始人走过这条路，根据我们从支持的数百家早期初创公司所看到的，通往极致产品市场契合度的旅程通常需要两到六年。

Our contention is that product market fit is not binary — and it's not something that you get to overnight, despite what all of those 'we just went viral' founding stories may have you believe.

我们的观点是，产品市场契合度不是二元的——也不是你一夜之间就能达到的，尽管所有那些我们就是走红了这样的创业故事可能让你相信这一点。

Once in a while, a company shoots up the ladder quickly, but that tends to be the exception. Some companies move up the progression over a longer period of time, while others skip ahead at different moments, leapfrogging from nascent to strong, for example.

偶尔，一家公司会迅速攀升，但这往往是例外。有些公司在更长的时间内逐步推进，而另一些公司在不同时刻跳跃前进，例如从初生跳到强劲。

We'll also note that the journey to PMF is a two-way ladder, meaning levels aren't permanently unlocked — it is possible to regress. To use another metaphor, think of it more as a dynamic equilibrium.

我们还要注意，通往PMF的旅程是一个双向阶梯，意味着层级不是永久解锁的——有可能倒退。用另一个比喻来说，把它想象成一个动态平衡。

We'll get deep into the details of what progressing through each level feels like further down, but staying at our current altitude, there's one high-level question you can ask here if you're unsure where your company stacks up: How hard is it to find and serve your next 'marginal' customer?

我们将深入探讨每个层级进展的感觉，但停留在当前高度，如果你不确定你的公司处于什么位置，有一个高层次的问题你可以问：找到并服务你的下一个边际客户有多难？

At Levels 3 and 4, for example, demand swells. Contrast that with Levels 1 and 2, where the search for the marginal customer feels more like a battle on several fronts — finding the right person, convincing them that your solution is what they need, converting them, and turning them into satisfied customers.

例如，在第3和第4层级，需求激增。相比之下，在第1和第2层级，寻找边际客户感觉更像是在多条战线上的战斗——找到合适的人，说服他们你的解决方案是他们需要的，转化他们，并让他们成为满意的客户。

产品市场契合度的三个维度

The 3 Dimensions of Product-Market Fit

We like to break that definition down even further into what we call the three dimensions of product-market fit:

我们喜欢将这个定义进一步分解为我们所说的产品市场契合度的三个维度：

• Satisfaction: How happy are customers with the product? Are you able to effectively retain customers? How much do people need the product? It may seem like splitting hairs, but we'll note that we've intentionally used 'need' and not 'love' here — there are many companies that have extreme PMF without customer love.

• 满意度：客户对产品有多满意？你能够有效地留住客户吗？人们有多需要这个产品？这可能看起来是在吹毛求疵，但我们要注意的是，我们在这里有意使用了需要而不是喜爱——有许多公司在没有客户喜爱的情况下拥有极致的PMF。

• Demand: Do you have the proverbial 'lines out the door'? How quickly and how much can you sell your product for?

• 需求：你有传说中的门外排长队吗？你能以多快的速度、多高的价格销售你的产品？

• Efficiency: Can the product grow repeatably and scale effectively? In our view, this is the critical piece that many definitions of PMF gloss over — even though it's one of the most important things that a company must achieve.

• 效率：产品能否可重复地增长并有效扩展？在我们看来，这是许多PMF定义所忽视的关键部分——尽管这是公司必须实现的最重要的东西之一。

Some may bristle at that last one. Most other definitions of product-market fit overtly focus on satisfaction and demand, implying that efficiency takes care of itself in the long run. But our belief is that while your business won't be efficient from day one — and may in fact be intentionally inefficient while you work to figure things out at Level 1 — there is tremendous value in building with an eye towards efficiency throughout your entire journey as a founder.

有些人可能对最后一点感到不满。大多数其他产品市场契合度的定义公开关注满意度和需求，暗示效率从长远来看会自行解决。但我们相信，虽然你的业务不会从第一天就高效——事实上，当你在Level 1努力解决问题时，可能是有意低效的——但在你作为创始人的整个旅程中，以效率为目标进行建设具有巨大的价值。

As a thought exercise, say you open up a stand on the side of the road where anyone who lines up and gives you $1 gets $100 in return. By classic definitions, product-market fit would be off the charts. You'd have lines out the door. Customers would be very satisfied. But it would be a mirage.

作为一个思维练习，假设你在路边开了一个摊位，任何排队给你1美元的人都会得到100美元的回报。按照经典定义，产品市场契合度将是超标的。你会门外排长队。客户会非常满意。但这将是一个海市蜃楼。

When you're starting a marathon, it's helpful to know what the final mile needs to look like — even if you don't alter your approach to mile 1.

当你开始一场马拉松时，知道最后一英里需要是什么样子是有帮助的——即使你不改变你对第一英里的方法。

First Round 4P：在通往产品市场契合度道路上摆脱困境的杠杆

The First Round 4Ps: Levers to Get Unstuck on Your Path to Product-Market Fit

As for how you can get unstuck and catapult your company to the next level, you can experiment with pulling these four levers at various points along your journey (our spin on the classic 4Ps framework):

至于如何摆脱困境并将你的公司推向下一层级，你可以在旅程的各个阶段尝试拉动这四个杠杆（我们对经典4P框架的诠释）：

• The persona: Who would benefit most from your insight? Do you have a person in a specific type of role or function who faces a set of challenges in mind? Example from Plaid: Consumers didn't need another budgeting app, but fintech apps needed the integrations the team had built on the back-end.

• 用户画像：谁最能从你的洞察中受益？你心中是否有一个特定类型角色或职能的人面临一系列挑战？来自Plaid的例子：消费者不需要另一个预算应用程序，但金融科技应用程序需要团队在后台构建的集成。

• The problem: Is this an urgent and important problem that your target persona has? It's the classic painkiller versus vitamin question. Example from Lattice: Stayed focused on people leaders, but went from building a solution for OKRs to solving performance management.

• 问题：这是你的目标用户画像面临的紧急且重要的问题吗？这是经典的止痛药与维生素问题。来自Lattice的例子：始终专注于人事领导者，但从为OKR构建解决方案转向解决绩效管理问题。

• The promise: Are those customers interested in what you'll do to address that need — your unique value proposition? Example from Ironclad: Repositioned from an AI legal assistant to a Contract Lifecycle Management platform.

• 承诺：这些客户是否对你将如何解决该需求感兴趣——你的独特价值主张？来自Ironclad的例子：从AI法律助手重新定位为合同生命周期管理平台。

• The product: Will the product that you're building actually deliver on this promise? Example from Alma: Pivoted during the pandemic from a community-based physical office concept for therapists, to giving providers a suite of digital tools.

• 产品：你正在构建的产品实际上会兑现这一承诺吗？来自Alma的例子：在疫情期间从为治疗师提供的社区型实体办公室概念转向为提供者提供一套数字工具。

极致产品市场契合度在现实世界中的样子

What Extreme Product-Market Fit Looks Like in the Real World

To bring this new concept of extreme product-market fit to life, let's walk through an example from Looker when it reached this Level 4 milestone as a Series C company with a team of 235 at the end of 2016 — after five years of hard work progressing through Levels 1-3.

为了让这个极致产品市场契合度的新概念生动起来，让我们看看Looker的例子，它在2016年底作为一家C轮公司，拥有235人的团队，经过五年努力从Level 1-3推进后，达到了这个Level 4里程碑。

Here's how Looker checked each box on our list of the three dimensions at this time:

以下是Looker当时如何在我们的三个维度清单上勾选每个框的：

• Demand: Went from 450 to nearly 800 customers in a year, growing revenue from $11.5M to $27M and increasing ACV to $57.7K.

• 需求：一年内从450个客户增加到近800个客户，收入从1150万美元增长到2700万美元，ACV增加到5.77万美元。

• Satisfaction: Extremely happy customers, with 141% NRR, a 18 month-streak of net negative churn and renewals higher than plan.

• 满意度：极度满意的客户，NRR达到141%，连续18个月实现净负流失，续约率高于计划。

• Efficient: The company had built an efficient model, with 77.6% gross margin.

• 效率：公司建立了高效的模型，毛利率达到77.6%。

Looker's path is a great example of each dimension coming together to contribute to extreme product-market fit. But just as there are success stories like these, in our experience, the absence of one or more of these dimensions is also the biggest reason that startups fail.

Looker的道路是一个很好的例子，说明每个维度如何结合起来促成极致产品市场契合度。但正如有这样的成功故事一样，根据我们的经验，缺少一个或多个这些维度也是初创公司失败的最大原因。

We believe that if you think about PMF in these terms, you'll be more likely to end up at strong or extreme levels of PMF than all the companies that didn't make it — in part because you and your co-founders will have a shared framework to more precisely diagnose where you might be falling short.

我们相信，如果你用这些术语来思考PMF，你比所有没有成功的公司更有可能最终达到强劲或极致的PMF水平——部分原因是你和你的联合创始人将有一个共享框架，可以更精确地诊断你可能在哪里做得不够。
