层级4：极致

Level 4: Extreme

Finally, Level 4: extreme product-market fit, which should be the goal of every startup.

最后，Level 4：极致产品市场契合度，这应该是每个初创公司的目标。

If you're at this stage, repeatability shines through in your ability to win over customers that are in your crisply defined target persona — it doesn't mean you have a 100% conversion rate, but there's tremendous consistency.

如果你处于这个阶段，可重复性在你赢得清晰定义的目标用户画像客户的能力中闪耀——这并不意味着你有100%的转化率，但有巨大的consistency。

This is also often where a company's brand often begins to inflect. With more widespread recognition, you may be on the cusp of becoming the 'Kleenex' of your category.

这也是公司品牌通常开始变化的阶段。随着更广泛的认可，你可能正处于成为你品类的舒洁的边缘。

感觉如何

What it Feels Like

我们听说过其他过早尝试的公司的恐怖故事。但我们得到了很好的建议，在你感觉像是在半速骑行之前，在你作为CEO对核心市场取得的所有进展感到满意之前，你不应该扩张。

We'd heard horror stories from other companies that tried too early. But we got good advice that you shouldn't expand until you feel like you're pedaling at half speed, until you as the CEO are comfortable with all the progress that is being made in your core market.

在公司建设的早期，你在骑自行车上坡，尽可能用力地踩踏板。当到了扩大你的TAM的时候，你在寻找那种感觉，你不是在下坡，而是在平坦的道路上，没有那么多阻力。

Earlier in company building, you're going uphill on a bike and pedaling as hard as you can. When it comes time to expand your TAM, you're looking for that feeling where you're not going downhill, but you're on a flat road and there isn't as much resistance.

看起来如何：极致需求、满意度、效率案例研究

What it Looks Like: A Case Study on Extreme Demand, Satisfaction, Efficiency in Action

Our example company for this level is called Metriq, a fictional intelligence platform that delivers self-service analytics and reports so teams can make decisions faster.

我们这个层级的示例公司叫做Metriq，一个虚构的智能平台，提供自助分析和服务，以便团队可以更快地做出决策。

They're at 186 employees and they just raised a $75 million Series C. Their burn multiple is looking very good at .87 — last month they brought in $2M of net-new ARR, and only burned $1.73M.

他们有186名员工，刚刚筹集了7500万美元的C轮融资。他们的燃烧倍数看起来非常好，为0.87——上个月他们带来了200万美元的净新增ARR，只消耗了173万美元。

Let's walk through each of the dimensions of product-market fit:

让我们逐一了解产品市场契合度的各个维度：

• Demand: They've got 262 customers. They're almost at $30M in ARR. Sales conversion is 24%. Their ACV is $112K. They grew ARR 3x over the prior 12 months.

• 需求：他们有262个客户。他们的ARR接近3000万美元。销售转化率为24%。他们的ACV是11.2万美元。他们在过去12个月里ARR增长了3倍。

• Satisfaction: Their customers are using the platform regularly and without prompting — the product is that important to them. They're at 123% NRR, and their regretted churn is only 6%.

• 满意度：他们的客户定期使用平台，无需提示——产品对他们如此重要。他们的NRR为123%，遗憾流失仅为6%。

• Efficiency: The sales, onboarding, and customer success teams are running smoothly. They've got repeatable playbooks. Gross margin is up to 86% and they're at an 11 month CAC payback.

• 效率：销售、入职和客户成功团队运行顺利。他们有可重复的操作手册。毛利率高达86%，CAC回收期为11个月。

最重要的是什么

What Matters Most

Whether it's Salesforce, Stripe, or Datadog, all the legendary enterprise companies have managed to enter multiple markets with multiple products and reach extreme PMF time and time again in new customer segments.

无论是Salesforce、Stripe还是Datadog，所有传奇的企业公司都成功地用多个产品进入多个市场，并在新的客户细分市场中一次又一次地达到极致PMF。

If you want your company to compound value, you're not done with growth in year six or seven, or just because you've reached a certain size. Growing over a long period of time is actually the act of finding extreme PMF over and over again.

如果你想让你的公司复利增值，你在第六年或第七年还没有完成增长，或者仅仅因为你达到了一定的规模。长期增长实际上是一遍又一遍地寻找极致PMF的行为。

我们在Atlassian的第二年创建了我们的第二个产品Confluence，这真的非常不寻常——尤其是当你有一个像Jira这样增长非常好的突破性产品时。

We created our second product, Confluence, in Atlassian's second year, which is really unusual — especially when you've got a breakout product like Jira that's growing really nicely.

在Atlassian非常早期的阶段，我们就开始训练我们的大脑思考公司随着规模扩大需要做的所有这些事情。

At a really early age at Atlassian, we began to wire our brain to think about all of those things that companies need to do as they get bigger.

Once you've reached extreme product-market fit and are ready to attempt to do it all over again, you can effectively expand your TAM — which is how you compound revenue — by remixing one (and eventually all) of three components: your product, your market or your buyer.

一旦你达到了极致产品市场契合度并准备再次尝试，你可以通过重新组合三个组件中的一个（最终是全部）来有效扩展你的TAM——这就是你如何复利收入的方式：你的产品、你的市场或你的买家。

That could look like: Creating new product use cases by adding new features and functionality. Creating a net-new product that you sell in the same market to the same buyer. Taking the same product and expanding into a different market. Selling the same product in the same market to a different buyer.

这可能看起来像是：通过添加新功能和功能创建新的产品用例。创建一个全新的产品，你在同一市场卖给同一个买家。采用相同的产品并扩展到不同的市场。在同一市场向不同的买家销售相同的产品。

It's like you're assembling a layer cake over many many years. You're adding on new features, new products, new customers, new market segments — trying to stack your way to a multi-billion dollar valuation without knocking the whole thing over.

就像你在很多很多年里组装一个千层蛋糕。你在添加新功能、新产品、新客户、新市场细分——试图在不把整个东西撞倒的情况下，堆叠出一条通往数十亿美元估值的道路。

尽管我现在经常开玩笑说Vanta的早期日子以及我们对所有不同的初创想法感到多么困惑，但事实是那段时期真的很艰难。

As much as I now joke about Vanta's early days and how confused we were with all of our different startup ideas, the truth is that period is really rough.

从外部看，你可以将创始人的生活浪漫化，并想，世界是你的牡蛎。你可以构建任何你想要的东西。这些都是真的，但在日常中，更像是，我在做什么？有人在乎吗？会有人在乎吗？

From the outside, you can romanticize a founder's life and think, 'The world's your oyster. You can build whatever you want.' And that's all true, but in the day-to-day, it's more like, 'What am I doing? Does anyone care? Will anyone ever care?'
